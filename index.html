Projects

Flash Mobs

A flash mob is when a group of people arrange to show up at the same place, ideally in high numbers. There are different kinds, but I participated in and then was a choreographer and coordinator of a few dozen mobs over 5 or 6 years.

Here are a few mobs that I'm proud of. Take a look at the first one, at least. I'd love to share them with you.

Walk to End Alzheimer's Mob

I think that this one is just gorgeous. The reflective purple surface of the Museum of Pop Culture in Seattle forms the backdrop and perfectly matches the purple theme of the walk.

Unfortunately, it won't embed. So, I just have to provide a link here instead.

Walk to End Alzheimer's Mob link

Marriage Proposal Mob - (Glee Flash Mob) - 2012

This is the 3rd annual Glee Flash Mob that took place in Seattle in 2012. We put out a message a few months before asking whether there was anyone who would be interested in using a flash mob as a proposal. The boyfriend of a long-time mobber, Emily, reached out and the plan was hatched.

During flash mob practices, we said that there would be a special guest and teased different celebrities, just saying that they'd finally see who it was once everyone turned to the center and crouched. It was no celebrity waiting there, but Tim. She said yes, by the way, and they were eventually married on the same spot.

If you watch, definitely start the video at 1 minute and 20 seconds. The beginning is a little rough. The proposal is at 3:39.

Final Glee Mob - 2014

For the fifth and final Glee Flash Mob, Beth Meberg and I, the choreographers, decided to just start the mob ourselves. Many of the dancers who joined just after that were from a dance group I danced (part-time) with for 10 years, Splinter Dance Company.

Some of this choreography was from previous Glee Mobs and I wasn't responsible for the choreo of the first two years.

Glee Mob Year 2 - 2012

I didn't actually participate in the choreography for this flash mob, though I do still appear in it. Like the other Glee Mobs, you can see me in orange.
